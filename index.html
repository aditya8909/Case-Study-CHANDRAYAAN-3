CHANDRAYAN 3

Chandrayaan-3 is a mission by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) to the moon that demonstrates the ability to safely land and rover on the moon's surface. The mission consists of a lander and rover configuration, with the lander able to soft land at a specified lunar site and deploy the rover to analyze the surface. The mission's primary objective is to soft land near the lunar south polar region and perform experiments using the instruments on Vikram and Pragyaan.

Vikram Lander

The Vikram lander is a miniature version of an LRA (Lunar Reconnaissance Array) that's part of the Chandrayaan-3 mission. It's the only LRA near the moon's south pole, and is designed to serve as a long-term geological station and location marker. The lander has a Laser Retroreflector Array (LRA) that NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center supplies, and that's used to help guide astronauts during future landings. The LRA also helps determine the spacecraft's orbital position, and can help refine the lunar geodetic frame.

Pragyaan Rover

The Pragyaan rover is a part of the Chandrayaan-3 spacecraft. The rover is solar-powered, weighs 27 kg, and is 0.9 m × 0.75 m × 0.85 m in size. It has six wheels and can travel at 1 cm per second, covering 500 m on the moon's surface. The rover is equipped with an alpha particle X-ray spectrometer (APXS) and Laser Induced Breakdown Spectroscope (LIBS) to perform chemical analysis of the lunar surface.

The Landing

On August 23, 2023, the Vikram lander landed near the moon's south pole, and has been operating scientific instruments for one lunar day. The lander also has payloads that measure the thermal conductivity and temperature of the moon's surface, seismic activity around the landing site, and plasma density. On September 4, 2023, ISRO put Pragyan and the lander into sleep mode before sunset, hoping to revive them during the next sunrise around September 22. However, as of October 2, 2023, the European station in Kourou and ISRO Telemetry, Tracking and Command Network (ISTRAC) in Bengaluru have been unable to revive the two.
